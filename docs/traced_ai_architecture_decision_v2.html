Architecture Decision Record — ADR-001

Traced AI — Core Architecture

audit & compliance infrastructure April 2026 v1.0

Traced AI captures the why behind every AI decision in production. Not the raw data — the proof it existed, when, under which rule, and who signed off. Built for regulated industries where "the AI decided" is not an acceptable answer.

DECISION 01

Raw I/O never leaves the client

The library writes actual inputs and outputs to a local SQLite store. Nothing crosses the network. "We can't see your data" is an architectural constraint, not a policy promise.

DECISION 02

Hashes go to the cloud ledger

SHA-256 hashes of every input and output, plus the rationale, are sent to Traced AI's append-only chained ledger. Independent tamper-evident proof. 32 bytes per event, not kilobytes.

DECISION 03

Dashboard ships as a Docker image

Enterprise clients run the full UI on their own infrastructure. It reads from local SQLite. No inbound ports. Outbound only: hashes to the ledger, rule updates from the registry.

DECISION 04

Rule registry stays in the cloud

The compliance intelligence, what to log for a loan decision vs. a medical extraction, which Article governs it, what format the auditor expects, is served on subscription. No subscription, no compliance artifact. Just a log.

The moat

The code is copyable. The Docker image is inspectable. The export format is reverse-engineerable. None of that is the moat. The moat is the versioned mapping of regulatory text to concrete logging requirements, built from real auditor interactions, updated as regulations move, and accepted by the notified bodies that sign off on conformity assessments. A competitor who copies the PDF layout still doesn't know what Bureau Veritas will push back on in a real EU AI Act review. That knowledge is the company.
